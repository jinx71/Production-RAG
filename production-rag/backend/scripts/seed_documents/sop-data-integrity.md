# SOP-DI-001: Data Integrity and ALCOA+ Principles

## 1. Purpose
This procedure establishes data integrity expectations for all GMP records, whether
paper, electronic, or hybrid, so that data supporting product quality decisions is
trustworthy and reliable.

## 2. ALCOA+ Principles
All GMP data must be:
- **Attributable:** it is clear who recorded the data and when.
- **Legible:** the data is readable and permanent.
- **Contemporaneous:** the data is recorded at the time the activity is performed.
- **Original:** the first capture of the data, or a certified true copy, is retained.
- **Accurate:** the data is correct and free from error.

The "plus" extends these with **Complete, Consistent, Enduring, and Available**.
Together these are referred to as the ALCOA+ principles and they apply to the entire
data lifecycle from generation through retention and retrieval.

## 3. Audit Trails
Electronic systems that create or modify GMP data must maintain a secure,
computer-generated, time-stamped audit trail that records the original entry, any
change, the previous value, the reason where required, and the identity of the person
making the change. Audit trails must not be disabled and must be reviewed as part of
batch record review for systems that support release decisions.

## 4. Access Control and Segregation of Duties
Each user must have a unique login that is never shared. System privileges are granted
on the principle of least privilege. A person who performs an activity should not be
the only person able to approve it; segregation of duties prevents a single
individual from both creating and approving the same critical record.

## 5. Static and Dynamic Records
A static record is a fixed-format record such as a printout. A dynamic record allows
interaction, such as a chromatography data file that can be reprocessed. Dynamic
records must be retained in their dynamic form so that the full context, including the
audit trail and metadata, is preserved; a printout alone is not sufficient for a
dynamic record.

## 6. Data Review and Retention
GMP data must be reviewed by a second qualified person before the associated batch is
released. Records must be retained for at least one year beyond the expiry of the
batch, or longer where local regulation requires. Backups must be verified for
readability, and the ability to restore data must be periodically tested.
