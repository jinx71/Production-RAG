# SOP-CAL-007: Calibration of Critical Process Instruments

## 1. Purpose
This procedure governs the calibration of instruments that measure or control
critical process parameters, ensuring measurements are traceable, accurate, and
within defined tolerance throughout the equipment lifecycle.

## 2. Instrument Classification
Instruments are classified by impact on product quality:
- **Critical:** directly affects a critical quality attribute or critical process
  parameter (for example, a temperature probe controlling a sterilisation cycle).
  Critical instruments require formal calibration with documented certificates.
- **Non-critical:** used for indication or general monitoring with no direct product
  impact. These may be verified rather than formally calibrated.

Only critical instruments fall under the full requirements of this SOP.

## 3. Calibration Tolerance and Acceptance
Each instrument has a defined acceptance tolerance derived from the process
requirement, not from the instrument specification alone. The calibration tolerance
must always be tighter than the process tolerance so that an in-tolerance instrument
guarantees the process stays within its allowed range.

A calibration passes when the as-found readings at all test points are within the
acceptance tolerance. The standard used to calibrate must have an accuracy at least
four times better than the tolerance of the instrument being calibrated; this is the
4:1 test accuracy ratio.

## 4. As-Found and As-Left Recording
Every calibration records both the **as-found** condition (the state of the
instrument before any adjustment) and the **as-left** condition (the state after
adjustment). As-found data is essential because an out-of-tolerance as-found result
triggers an impact assessment on all product manufactured since the last successful
calibration.

## 5. Calibration Interval
Calibration intervals are set based on instrument stability, criticality, and
historical drift. A new critical instrument is typically assigned a six-month
interval, which may be extended or shortened based on documented calibration history.
Intervals are reviewed at least annually.

## 6. Out-of-Tolerance Handling
When an instrument is found out of tolerance:
1. Quarantine the instrument and label it as out of service.
2. Raise a deviation under SOP-DEV-003.
3. Perform an impact assessment covering batches affected since the last good
   calibration.
4. Re-calibrate or replace the instrument before returning it to service.

Traceability to national or international measurement standards must be maintained
for every standard used in calibration.
